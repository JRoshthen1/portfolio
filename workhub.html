Report of my journey


First day after summer, we received an assignment with the first showcase of generating ideas for the next 7 weeks which sounded a lot easier than it actually was. These brainstorm sessions were very scary because we had very little time to think of all the variables in business, thus generate a good idea that we will enjoy building for quite some time. We all had super different ideas and a lot of them were very good ones but through some research and finding what are some of 2021 prob- lems we figured out that we could help people find just some simple work or make some quick money with their skills. Finding people for discussing this kind of problem with was very easy. So many people are still suffering from the pandemic or just want to make some money so the incentive of using our product exists. We mostly focused on social media posts and analyzed them for the intelligence of what people are looking for, how they are searching for it, and what platforms they are using. Creating personas was a really interesting process in this assignment because we had so many building blocks that we could use to get it just right, but sometimes too much infor- mation can be difficult to handle. Competitive analysis was also very important for designing our product and making it user-friendly. Our main competitor was a platform called Workee. We sat down and wrote down all the things that stand out on their app and on the landing page which helped us to create the basic structure of our app. By this time I was watching hours of tutorials and courses for Vue, Firebase, Vuex, and Express. Which I realized later in this project, was not very good practice. Before the first milestone, we had a week of Portrait photography. I really enjoyed this week but I found that most of the things we learned were not really useful to us. We shot some portraits of people doing some simple tasks which we could use in our social media presence but overall information about product design and taking professional business photographs that could be helpful in our product was absent. After the photography lessons, we started learning about some new CSS frameworks that could be used in our project. I took a lot from these classes, I never realized there is a variety of interesting frameworks to choose from. But we still stuck with the good old Bootstrap. As mentioned, all the tutorials and courses for Vue and Firebase got me confused. So I started to read the documentation very carefully and tried to get my logic frame worked. Firebase is a very interesting piece of software for many reasons. For us, the main reason was the pricing and Google connection. Because Firebase is made by Google it was easy hooking up Google sign-in and accessing the Authentication database. But most of our problems came with communicating with other databases. I was very confused about the terminology such as “keys, mount, async, and await...” from watching all the videos. They gave me all the tools I required but without understanding them in-depth, it was impossi- ble to implement them. Testing our prototypes was very fun in this project, unlike some other ones. We really tried to build something that helps people so people were happy to test it and point out a lot of stuff that we would surely miss. My overall conclusion is that I really enjoyed working on something that is ours, has a business plan that we can personalize, and is a great group to work with! I learned a lot of new things in this project from handling large amounts of data to coding in a couple of new frameworks. Every step of this project taught me to look at things from a different perspective which I am really grateful for.
