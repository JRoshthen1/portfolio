Report of my journey


First day after summer, we received an assignment with the first showcase of generating ideas for the next 7 weeks, which sounded a lot easier than it actually was. These brainstorm sessions were very scary because we had very little time to think of all the variables in business, thus generate a good idea that we will enjoy building for quite some time. We all had super different ideas and a lot of them were very good ones but through some research and finding what are some of the 2021 problems we figured out that we could help people find just some simple work or make some quick money with their skills.
Finding people for discussing this kind of problem with was very easy. So many people are still suffering from the pandemic or just want to make some money, so the incentive of using our product exists. We mostly focused on social media posts and analyzed them for the intelligence of what people are looking for, how they are searching for it and what platforms are they using. Creating personas was a really interesting process in this assignment because we had so many building blocks that we could use to get it just right, but sometimes too much information can be difficult to handle.
Competitive analysis was also very important for designing our product and making it user-friendly. Our main competitor was a platform called Workee, on which we made SWOT analysis.
For technologies, we stuck with the good old Bootstrap to strap everything together. Firebase is a very interesting piece of software for many reasons. For us, the main reason was the pricing and Google connection. Because Firebase is made by Google it was easy hooking up Google sign-in and accessing the Authentication database. But most of our problems came with communicating with other databases. I was very confused about the terminology such as “keys, mount, async, and await...” from watching all the tutorials. They gave me all the tools I required but without understanding them in-depth, it was impossi- ble to implement them.
Testing our prototypes was very fun in this project, unlike some other ones. We really tried to build something that helps people so people were happy to test it and point out a lot of stuff that we would surely miss. My overall conclusion is that I really enjoyed working on something that is ours, has a business plan that we can personalize, and is a great group to work with! I learned a lot of new things in this project from handling large amounts of data to coding in a couple of new frameworks. Every step of this project taught me to look at things from a different perspective which I am really grateful for.
